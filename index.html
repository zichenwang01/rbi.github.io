Abstract

Computing visibility-related derivatives remains the main source of difficulty in presently used physically based differentiable rendering methods. Recent unbiased estimators rely on elaborate guiding data structures or reparameterizations with a global impact on variance. All of them contribute significant architectural complexity.

In this article, we investigate an alternative that embraces nonzero bias in exchange for low variance and architectural simplicity. Our Relaxed Boundary Integral expands the lower-dimensional boundary integral into a thin band that is easy to sample when the underlying surface is represented by a Signed Distance Function (SDF). We demonstrate the performance and robustness of our relaxed formulation in end-to-end inverse rendering tasks, where it obtains results that are competitive or superior to existing work.
